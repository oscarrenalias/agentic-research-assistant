I'm writing an article to share my experience with large enterprise transformations, specifically focused on the main challenges (and potential solutions) that companies typically find.

Intended audience it senior IT leaders and professionals currently delivering transformations, or planning to deliver one.

The expected outcome is a long-form article, which can be as long as it needs to be. The article should contain the following:

- Introduction with the problem statement and context

- Recommendations based on my personal experience

The content should be structured using medium-length paragraphs, in plain language (it's ok to use enterprise terminology and jargon). Should also use headers to separate sections and sub-sections.

# Key points to consider

## Pitfalls
- organizational friction and pushback
- skill gap -- is your organization skilled enough to manage the change? to run your systems once transformed?
- transformation exhaustion - people eventually get tired when transforamtions run for too long
- "lift and shift" gets you there quickly, but does not deliver sufficient benefits
- framing the transformation as an IT program only, when it reality it should be a business transforamtion first and IT transformation second

## Recommendations
- keep the transformation aligned to business objectives and strategy
- enable change management early on, in order to remove organizational friction and pushback
- align the operating model with the transformation
- continuosly monitor value -- e.g., are we seeing the expected benefits? are we transforming things in the right priority?
- establish a strong portfolio view of the transformation with end-to-end ownership and accountability 
- strong top-down leadership support
